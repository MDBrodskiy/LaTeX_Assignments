\begin{enumerate}

  \item Impact of Monetary Policy

    \begin{itemize}

      \item Interest rates matter

      \item The Money Market

        \begin{itemize}

          \item Transaction Demand: NOT a function of interest rates, but a function of economic activity (\textit{i.e.\ money as a medium of exchange})

          \item Asset Demand: Money as a store of value. The opportunity cost of holding money is the interest rate you could be earning. Erosion due to inflation is another cost

        \end{itemize}

      \item The Money Market Model

    \begin{center}
      \begin{tikzpicture}
        \begin{axis}[xmin=0, ymin=0, xmax=10, title={Market for Loanable Funds}, xlabel={Quantity of Loanable Funds}, ylabel={Real Interest Rate}, ymajorgrids=true, xmajorgrids=true, grid style=dashed]
          \addplot[domain=1:9,blue] {10 - x};
          \addplot[domain=1:9,green] {x};
          \addplot[domain=0:5,yellow,dashed] {5};
          \node[label={180:{$D_{lf}$}},circle,fill,inner sep=2pt, color=blue] at (axis cs:9,1) {};
          \node[label={180:{$S_{lf}$}},circle,fill,inner sep=2pt, color=green] at (axis cs:9,9) {};
          \node[label={45:{$Q_{lf}$}},circle,fill,inner sep=2pt, color=yellow] at (axis cs:5,0) {};
          \node[label={45:{$i_{f}$}},circle,fill,inner sep=2pt, color=yellow] at (axis cs:0,5) {};
          \addplot[yellow,dashed] coordinates {(5,0) (5,5)};
        \end{axis}
      \end{tikzpicture}
    \end{center}

      \item Nominal Interest Rate vs. Quantity of Money — The graph is downward-sloping

      \item The nominal interest rate $i$ is the Federal Funds rate

      \item Only the Fed can shift the supply of money

      \item Shifters of transaction demand

        \begin{itemize}

          \item Changes in GDP

          \item Changes in price level

          \item Changes in technology

        \end{itemize}

    \end{itemize}

  \item Loanable Funds — All money available for lending and borrowing

  \item Uses for loanable funds (demand):

    \begin{itemize}

      \item Business investment

      \item Government budget deficit

      \item International investment or lending

    \end{itemize}

  \item What can shift the demand curve?

    \begin{itemize}

      \item Changes in expected profit from new capital

        \begin{itemize}

          \item Business cycle expansions

          \item Swings of optimism and pessimism

        \end{itemize}

      \item Investment incentives

      \item Government budget deficits

    \end{itemize}

  \item What can shift the supply curve?

    \begin{itemize}

      \item Savings incentives

        \begin{itemize}

          \item Taxes on interest

        \end{itemize}

      \item Expansionary monetary policy\footnote{May shift supply, but not always}

      \item Capital inflows

    \end{itemize}

\end{enumerate}
